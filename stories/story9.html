🐌 The Snail Who Raced a Shooting Star

In the garden of Twinkleleaf, there lived a tiny snail named Zoom. Zoom wasn’t like other snails. While others enjoyed slow walks and quiet naps, Zoom dreamed of speed.

“One day,” Zoom whispered to a ladybug, “I’ll go so fast, I’ll race a shooting star!”

The bugs laughed kindly. “That’s a lovely dream,” said a butterfly. “But stars are in the sky, and you… well, you're down here.”

But Zoom believed. Every day he trained. He slid on buttered leaves, zipped down dewy branches, and even did push-ups with his eyestalks.

One night, a glowing comet named Starley zipped low through the sky. She heard Zoom’s wish on the wind. She swirled down and hovered above the garden.

“You want to race me, little one?” Starley asked gently.

Zoom blinked. “Yes. Just once. I know I’m slow, but I believe in trying big things.”

Starley twinkled brightly. “Then let’s do it. We race from this mushroom to that mountain top.”

The whole garden came to watch. Ants waved flags made of rose petals. Frogs made trumpet sounds. Even the clouds held still.

“Ready, set, GLOW!” shouted a firefly.

Starley zoomed up, lighting the sky with rainbow trails. Zoom pushed forward, inch by inch, shell glowing with effort. He wasn’t fast, but he never stopped.

Hours passed. Starley finished first, of course. But when Zoom reached the top—every creature cheered. Starley herself floated down and placed a shiny star sticker on his shell.

“You didn’t win the race,” she said, “but you won something greater—you inspired the whole garden.”

From that night on, Zoom was known as the bravest snail in the stars. And whenever a shooting star flew across the sky, everyone whispered, “That’s Starley, visiting her favorite racing snail.”
