🐉 The Cupcake-Breathing Dragon

Deep in the land of Crumbletop, where volcanoes whistled and gumdrop trees grew wild, there lived a dragon named Dave. But Dave wasn’t your ordinary roar-and-fire kind of dragon. No, Dave had a very special quirk: instead of breathing fire, he breathed cupcakes.

It started when he was a hatchling. One sneeze and out popped a blueberry muffin with extra glitter. Another hiccup? A flying chocolate chip cupcake that sang lullabies. His parents, who were traditional lava-blasters, were baffled.

“He’s sweet!” his mom declared. “Literally!” said his dad, licking frosting from a tree.

Dave became famous across the land. Villagers would line up near his cave not with swords—but with forks. He could bake vanilla swirl, strawberry sparkle, even lemon-zap surprise with a single puff. His cave smelled like a dessert shop and was guarded not by treasure—but by frosting gnomes.

But one day, a mysterious baker named Madame Crustina arrived. She wore a hat shaped like a pie and had a rolling pin that glowed. “I hear you’re the dragon with the dessert breath,” she said, squinting.

Dave nodded politely and sneezed a caramel puff. It floated past her nose.

Crustina gasped. “That… smells like victory!” She had a wicked plan: steal Dave’s recipe and make millions of magic cupcakes without him!

That night, she crept into his cave with a frosting vacuum and a spellbook. But when she turned the pages, a cupcake trap popped open! It shot her straight into a jelly trampoline and bounced her into a marshmallow bush. Dave snored gently nearby, unaware of the chaos.

The next morning, Dave woke to find Crustina stuck to a candy cane. “Trying to steal secret recipes?” he asked.

She sighed, “I just wanted to be as magical as you.”

Dave smiled kindly. “Magic tastes better when shared.” And with that, he taught her how to sneeze cinnamon properly. They opened a bakery together called **"Flame & Frosting"**, and it became the coziest, happiest shop in Crumbletop.

And so, the dragon who breathed cupcakes didn’t just sweeten the skies—he sweetened hearts too.
